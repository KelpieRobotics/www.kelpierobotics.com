Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is an ROV?

    An ROV is a Remotely Operated Vehicle which is normally sent into environments that a person can not go to. This could be anywhere from the darkest depths of the ocean to the surface of Mars. It consists of a robotic vehicle that is designed to be controlled by a human, and is typically equiped with video cameras, and mechanical attachments to accomplish the designated task.

  • What competition does Kelpie Compete in?

    Keplie Robotics competes in the MATE ROV competition which is designed to get students to think and apply STEM concepts in a real life scenaria. This is done through a specially crafted set of tasks that the teams need to plan around, and build their rov for.

  • What is the purpose of the ROV?

    The purpose of the ROV is to allow a person to explore places that are dangerous to humans. This makes it safe for exploration since the human pilot will never be in the area of danger, but will instead be piloting the ROV from a distance. In this case, that would be underwater. An enviroment that is dangerous to people for obvious reasons.
